# Business Insights – Telco Customer Churn Analysis

## Executive Summary

This project analyzes customer churn in a telecom company using
Excel dashboards and Python machine learning.

The analysis identifies major factors contributing to customer churn
and proposes strategies to reduce customer attrition.

Dataset Size: 7043 customers

---

## Key Findings

### 1. High Churn in Month-to-Month Contracts
Customers with month-to-month contracts show the highest churn rate
(~43%) compared to long-term contracts.

### 2. Early Customer Churn
Customers within the first 12 months have the highest probability
of leaving the service.

### 3. Impact of Tech Support
Customers without TechSupport churn significantly more than those
with support services.

### 4. Pricing Sensitivity
Customers with higher monthly charges tend to churn more frequently.

### 5. Fiber Optic Customers
Fiber optic users show higher churn compared to DSL users.

---

## Machine Learning Insights

A Random Forest classification model was used to predict customer churn.

Model Performance:

Accuracy: ~81%  
ROC-AUC Score: ~0.84

The model identified approximately 280–300 customers with a high
probability of churn.

---

## Business Recommendations

Based on the analysis, the following strategies are recommended:

1. Encourage customers to switch to annual contracts.
2. Offer bundled TechSupport services.
3. Provide loyalty rewards for customers after the first year.
4. Monitor customers with high monthly charges.
5. Launch proactive retention campaigns for high-risk customers.

---

## Conclusion

Combining Excel-based analysis with machine learning predictions
provides a powerful approach to understanding customer churn.

These insights help businesses reduce customer loss and improve
long-term revenue retention.
